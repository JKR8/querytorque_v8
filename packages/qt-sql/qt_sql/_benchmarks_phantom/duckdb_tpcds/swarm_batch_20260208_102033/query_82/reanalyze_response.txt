FAILURE_ANALYSIS:
All attempts fell short because they focused on CTE-based pre-filtering without addressing the fundamental join explosion problem. The query joins three fact-like tables (inventory, store_sales) through item with no selective constraints on store_sales. This creates a multiplicative explosion: each item meeting inventory conditions can have thousands of store_sales records. Worker 3's 1.21x gain came from pre-joining inventory with date_dim but still hit the same explosion when joining with store_sales. Worker 4's structural changes (0.45x) added unnecessary complexity with UNIONs without reducing the underlying cartesian effect. Worker 5's EXISTS attempted to address this but retained DISTINCT operations on large intermediate sets. The optimizer likely already pushes predicates, making CTE isolation ineffective without changing the join semantics.
