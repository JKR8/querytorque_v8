### Step 2 — Design Targets  
**Primary Bottleneck**: Repeated full scans of large dimension tables (`date_dim`, `promotion`, `item`) across all 3 CTEs. The plan shows:  
- `date_dim` scanned 3 times (rows=10,16,16)  
- `promotion` scanned 3 times (rows=11 each)  
- `item` scanned 3 times with expensive `Index Scan` (time=0.005ms per scan)  

**Targets**:  
1. **Rescue Family E (Materialization)**  
   - Fix literal mismatch in `t1` by preserving original filters  
   - Precompute filtered dimensions once to avoid 9 redundant scans  
   - Targets: `date_dim` (scanned 3x), `promotion` (scanned 3x), `item` (scanned 3x)  

2. **Family F (Join Transform)**  
   - Restructure comma joins to explicit `INNER JOIN` syntax  
   - Enforce optimal join order: fact tables → dimensions  
   - Targets: `Nested Loop` in `ssr` (time=42.935ms) and `csr` (time=33.852ms)  

3. **Family A + E (Early Filtering + Materialization)**  
   - Push `i_category`/`i_current_price` filters into `item` CTE  
   - Combine with precomputed dimensions to minimize rows early  
   - Targets: `Index Scan on item` (rows=0, repeated 3x)  

4. **Novel: Aggregation Pushdown (Family C)**  
   - Aggregate `store_sales` before joining to `store_returns`  
   - Valid since `ss_item_sk`/`ss_ticket_number` ⊇ join keys  
   - Targets: `Nested Loop` feeding `Aggregate` in `ssr` (time=42.981ms)
