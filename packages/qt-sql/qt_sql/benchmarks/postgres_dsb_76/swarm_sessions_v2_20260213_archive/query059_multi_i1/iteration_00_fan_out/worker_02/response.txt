## Part 1: Modified Logic Tree

```
QUERY: (single statement)
├── [CTE] filtered_dates  [+]
│   ├── SCAN (date_dim)
│   ├── FILTER (d_month_seq BETWEEN 1187 AND 1210)
│   └── OUTPUT (d_date_sk, d_week_seq, d_month_seq, d_day_name)
├── [CTE] wss_both  [+]
│   ├── SCAN (store_sales)
│   ├── JOIN (filtered_dates ON d_date_sk = ss_sold_date_sk)
│   ├── FILTER (ss_sales_price / ss_list_price BETWEEN 0.57 AND 0.77)
│   ├── AGG (GROUP BY d_week_seq, ss_store_sk, d_month_seq)
│   └── OUTPUT (d_week_seq, ss_store_sk, d_month_seq, sun_sales..sat_sales)
├── [CTE] pivoted  [+]
│   ├── SCAN (wss_both)
│   ├── JOIN (store ON ss_store_sk = s_store_sk)
│   ├── FILTER (s_state IN ('AR','CO','IA','IL','NC','NY','PA','TX'))
│   ├── AGG (GROUP BY s_store_id, s_store_name, d_week_seq)
│   └── OUTPUT (s_store_id, s_store_name, d_week_seq, sun_sales1..sat_sales2)
└── [MAIN] main_query  [~]
    ├── SCAN (pivoted AS p1, pivoted AS p2)
    ├── JOIN (p1.s_store_id = p2.s_store_id AND p1.d_week_seq = p2.d_week_seq - 52)
    ├── SORT (s_store_name1 ASC, s_store_id1 ASC, d_week_seq1 ASC)
    ├── LIMIT (100)
    └── OUTPUT (s_store_name1, s_store_id1, d_week_seq1, sun_sales1/sun_sales2..sat_sales1/sat_sales2)
```

Changes: Replaced original self-join pattern with shared materialization of date-filtered store_sales, then pivoted both periods via conditional aggregation in a single pass, maintaining exact join semantics and division guards.

Expected speedup: ~3-4x due to eliminating duplicate fact-table scans and reducing join cardinality.
