## Part 1: Modified Logic Tree

```
QUERY: (single statement)
├── [CTE] returns_cte (formerly customer_total_return) [~] (explicit JOIN syntax, same logic)
│   ├── SCAN (catalog_returns, date_dim (join), customer_address AS returning_addr (join))
│   ├── JOIN (cr_returned_date_sk = d_date_sk)
│   ├── JOIN (cr_returning_addr_sk = returning_addr.ca_address_sk)
│   ├── FILTER (d_year = 2000)
│   ├── AGG (GROUP BY cr_returning_customer_sk, returning_addr.ca_state)
│   └── OUTPUT (ctr_customer_sk, ctr_state, ctr_total_return)
├── [CTE] state_avg_cte [+] (new CTE for state averages)
│   ├── SCAN (returns_cte)
│   ├── AGG (GROUP BY ctr_state)
│   └── OUTPUT (ctr_state, state_avg)
├── [CTE] filtered_customers [+] (new CTE for customers meeting threshold and IA filter)
│   ├── SCAN (returns_cte AS ctr1, state_avg_cte, customer)
│   ├── JOIN (ctr1.ctr_state = state_avg_cte.ctr_state AND ctr1.ctr_total_return > state_avg_cte.state_avg * 1.2)
│   ├── JOIN (ctr1.ctr_customer_sk = c_customer_sk)
│   ├── FILTER (EXISTS (SELECT 1 FROM customer_address WHERE ca_address_sk = c_current_addr_sk AND ca_state = 'IA'))
│   └── OUTPUT (c_customer_sk, ctr_total_return)
├── [MAIN] main_query [~] (now LATERAL join on address)
│   ├── SCAN (filtered_customers)
│   ├── LATERAL (SELECT * FROM customer_address WHERE ca_address_sk = c_current_addr_sk) AS ca
│   ├── JOIN LATERAL (ON true)
│   ├── SORT (by all 15 output columns)
│   └── OUTPUT (c_customer_id, c_salutation, c_first_name, c_last_name, ca_street_number, ca_street_name, ca_street_type, ca_suite_number, ca_city, ca_county, ca_state, ca_zip, ca_country, ca_gmt_offset, ca_location_type, ctr_total_return)
└── [LIMIT] limit 100 [=]
```

**Changes:** Decorrelated the state-average subquery into a separate CTE (`state_avg_cte`) and used an EXISTS filter for Iowa current addresses, then joined LATERAL to fetch address details only for the filtered small set.  

**Expected speedup:** ~2-3x from eliminating per-row correlated subquery execution and reducing address joins via late binding.
