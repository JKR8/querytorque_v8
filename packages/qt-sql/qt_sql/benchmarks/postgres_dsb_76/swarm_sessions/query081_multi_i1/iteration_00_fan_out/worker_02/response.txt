## Part 1: Modified Logic Tree

```
QUERY: (single statement)
├── [CTE] date_cte  [+]  Cost: 5%  Rows: ~365
│   └── SCAN (date_dim) FILTER (d_year = 2000) OUTPUT (d_date_sk)
├── [CTE] return_addr_cte  [+]  Cost: 10%  Rows: ~1000
│   └── SCAN (customer_address) OUTPUT (ca_address_sk, ca_state)
├── [CTE] returns_cte  [~]  Cost: 35%  Rows: ~1000
│   ├── SCAN (catalog_returns)  [=]
│   ├── JOIN (catalog_returns × date_cte) ON (cr_returned_date_sk = d_date_sk)  [!]
│   ├── JOIN (catalog_returns × return_addr_cte) ON (cr_returning_addr_sk = ca_address_sk)  [!]
│   ├── FILTER (d_year = 2000)  [=]
│   ├── AGG (GROUP BY cr_returning_customer_sk, ca_state)  [=]
│   └── OUTPUT (ctr_customer_sk, ctr_state, ctr_total_return)  [=]
├── [CTE] main_prefetch  [~]  Cost: 40%  Rows: ≤100
│   ├── SCAN (returns_cte AS ctr1, returns_cte AS ctr2, customer, customer_address)  [!]
│   ├── JOIN (ctr1 × ctr2) ON (ctr1.ctr_state = ctr2.ctr_state)  [!]
│   ├── JOIN (ctr1 × customer) ON (ctr1.ctr_customer_sk = c_customer_sk)  [!]
│   ├── JOIN (customer × customer_address) ON (ca_address_sk = c_current_addr_sk)  [!]
│   ├── FILTER (ctr1.ctr_total_return > AVG(ctr2.ctr_total_return) * 1.2)  [!]
│   ├── FILTER (ca_state = 'IA')  [=]
│   ├── AGG (GROUP BY ctr1.ctr_customer_sk, ctr1.ctr_state, ctr1.ctr_total_return, ...)  [!]
│   └── OUTPUT (c_customer_id, c_salutation, c_first_name, c_last_name, ..., ctr_total_return)  [=]
└── [MAIN] final_sort  [=]  Cost: 10%  Rows: ≤100
    └── SORT+LIMIT (ORDER BY all 16 columns, LIMIT 100)  [=]
```

**Changes:**
- `date_cte` and `return_addr_cte`: Added as prefetched dimension CTEs
- `returns_cte`: Changed comma joins to explicit JOIN syntax with CTEs
- `main_prefetch`: Changed comma joins to explicit JOIN syntax, moved correlated subquery logic to GROUP BY/HAVING
- Structural: Converted correlated subquery to self-join with aggregation

**Changes:** The rewrite pre-filters dimension tables into CTEs (date_dim for year 2000, all customer addresses) and converts all joins to explicit INNER JOIN syntax. The correlated subquery is transformed into a self-join between two instances of the returns CTE with GROUP BY/HAVING to compute state averages. This preserves the exact semantics while enabling better join planning with small dimension hash tables.

**Expected speedup:** ~2-3x from dimension prefetching and explicit join syntax, similar to pg_dimension_prefetch_star (3.32x) pattern. The correlated subquery elimination should help, though some overhead remains from the self-join aggregation.
