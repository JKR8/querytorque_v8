Looking at the query, I see a multi-table join with two date_dim instances (d1 and d2). The main cost comes from scanning large fact tables (store_sales and store_returns). The key insight is that we can prefetch and pre-filter the date dimensions, then join them with their respective fact tables early to reduce the intermediate result sizes.

The query has:
- d1 (ss_sold_date_sk) with no filter
- d2 (sr_returned_date_sk) with filters: d_year=2001 AND d_moy=8
- store with no explicit filter but used in GROUP BY

Strategy: Create CTEs for filtered d2 and pre-join it with store_returns, and create a CTE for store_sales joined with d1. This reduces the fact tables before the final join.
